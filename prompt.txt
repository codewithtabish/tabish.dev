  <section class="space-y-5">
    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold underline decoration-2 decoration-indigo-500 dark:decoration-indigo-400">
      What Are Large Language Models? ⭑⭑
    </h2>
    <p class="text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
      Large Language Models, or LLMs, are advanced neural networks trained on massive text datasets to understand and generate natural language. 
      They predict the next word in a sequence based on context and probability, enabling them to produce coherent and contextually accurate text. 
      These models are built using architectures like <span class="font-semibold text-indigo-600 dark:text-indigo-400">Transformers</span>, 
      which have become the backbone of modern AI systems.
    </p>
  </section>

  <!-- Section 2 -->
  <section class="space-y-5">
    <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold underline decoration-2 decoration-indigo-500 dark:decoration-indigo-400">
      How Do LLMs Learn Language? ⭑⭑
    </h2>
    <p class="text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
      LLMs learn by analyzing billions of text samples — books, articles, conversations, and code — to identify language patterns. 
      During training, they break text into smaller units called <span class="font-semibold text-indigo-600 dark:text-indigo-400">tokens</span>. 
      Each token represents a piece of a word or phrase. The model then learns relationships between tokens through multi-layered 
      neural computations.
    </p>
  </section>

      The Role of Transformers ⭑⭑
    </h2>
    <p class="text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
      The <span class="font-semibold text-indigo-600 dark:text-indigo-400">Transformer</span> architecture revolutionized natural language 
      processing. It uses mechanisms called <span class="italic">attention</span> and <span class="italic">self-attention</span> to determine 
      which parts of the input text are most relevant for generating accurate outputs. This design allows LLMs to understand long-range 
      dependencies between words — even across entire paragraphs.
    </p>

    <p class="text-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
